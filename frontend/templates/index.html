Project Overview

AI-Driven Geospatial Modeling and Risk Mapping

Localized respiratory and skin health risk estimation using satellite, ground, and GIS-integrated air pollution data — Karnataka, India (2020–2025)
This system estimates environmental health risk only. It does not perform medical diagnosis.

Obj 1 — Multi-Source Data Integration

CPCB Ground Data

  • PM2.5, PM10, NO2, O3
  • Source: CPCB Stations
  • Coverage: 13 districts
  • Date range: 2020–2025

Satellite Data

  • NO2, O3, SO2, CO, UV Aerosol Index, AOD, PM2.5 est.
  • Source: GEE / Sentinel-5P
  • Resolution: 3.5km
  • Daily global coverage

GIS Contextual Data

  • Population density, road density
  • Land use type
  • Urban/rural classification
  • Industrial zones, green cover
  • District area (GADM shapefile)

District Coverage Map

Data Fusion Pipeline

1
Raw CPCB CSV files cleaned and standardized
2
Sentinel-5P NetCDF4 downloaded and converted
3
Spatial alignment to 1km grid
4
GIS contextual features joined by district
5
Unified pollutant columns created (ground + satellite fallback)
6
Final enriched dataset: 65,760 rows × 38 columns
